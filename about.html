The Story

Why this exists

I didn't come to this work through a course or a qualification. I came through curiosity, through my own body, and through years of noticing what touch — real touch — can do.

I grew up naturist. Nakedness was never loaded with shame in our household — it was simply the state of being human. That foundational ease with the unclothed body never left me, and it shapes everything I bring to a session.

What I found, over time, was that most people carry an almost unbearable amount of tension they've long since stopped noticing. In the chest. In the hips. In the jaw. Not from injury — from life. From the accumulated effort of holding themselves together.

Touch, offered slowly and without agenda, has a way of making space for that to ease. It isn't magic. It doesn't fix anything. But it can offer the body a genuine experience of being held — and for many people, that is rarer than it should be.

On being comfortable with all of you

I am equally comfortable working with men and women. I carry no assumptions about what bodies need or want based on gender. The same quality of attention, the same quality of care, the same unhurried presence — for everyone.

I am drawn to people who carry depth. Who are curious about their own inner life. Who can tolerate the kind of stillness that allows sensation to speak. You don't need to know what you're looking for — only that something has called you here.

On consent and care

Every session begins with a conversation — not a questionnaire, a genuine exchange. What you're carrying. What feels tender. What feels numb. What you'd like more of. What you'd prefer I stay away from.

Consent is continuous here. Not a checkbox at the start, but something that breathes through the entire session. You can change your mind. You can pause. You can ask for something different. That is not an interruption — it is exactly how this is meant to work.

My role is not to lead you somewhere. It is to be present enough that the work can go wherever it needs to go.

"The body does not lie about what it needs. Most of us have simply learned not to listen."
